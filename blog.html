The Authenticity Protocol

Inverting the Burden of Proof

Abstract: In an era where generative AI can synthesize reality with near-perfect fidelity, existing verification frameworks—watermarking and detection—are fundamentally flawed. They place the burden of proof on the detector, an arms race that defenders are destined to lose.

This whitepaper proposes the Authenticity Protocol, a cryptographic framework that inverts this paradigm. Instead of detecting fakes, we must cryptographically bind identity to origin at the point of creation.

Core Principles

  • Identity-Bound Creation: Every piece of content is signed by a verified identity key.
  • Chain of Custody: Modifications are appended to a ledger, preserving the original provenance.
  • Default Distrust: Any unsigned content is treated as synthetic by default.

Implementation Strategy

We leverage decentralized identity (DID) standards combined with hardware-backed signing (e.g., C2PA) to create a tamper-evident media ecosystem. This shifts the verification cost from complex, unreliable AI detection models to deterministic cryptographic checks.
